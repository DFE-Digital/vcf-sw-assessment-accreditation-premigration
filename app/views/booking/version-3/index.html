Social worker assessment and accreditation

Overview

The social worker assessment enables child and family social workers at practitioner level to develop skills and knowledge to improve outcomes for children and families.

The assessment

The social worker assessment has two parts to it:

  • knowledge assessment
  • simulated practice assessment

You will take both parts of the assessment online.

Youâ€™ll take the knowledge assessment before you take the simulated scenario assessment.

Knowledge assessment

The knowledge assessment is a multiple-choice assessment.

You will have 1 hour 30 minutes to answer 60 multiple-choice questions.

The assessment includes short general knowledge questions based on the practitioner PQS.

Simulated practice assessment

Two simulated practice scenarios (like a role play) each 15 minutes long.

It will be a simulated meeting between you and an actor trained to play the role of either a:

  • family member
  • young person
  • social worker
  • other professional

You will then complete two further assessments based on your simulated practice scenario:

  • 1 reflective assessment, 15 minutes long, based on the first scenario
  • 1 written assessment, 30 minutes long, based on the second scenario

A reflective assessment

The reflective assessment is a 15 minute verbal reflection based on the first simulated practice scenario you completed.

A written assessment

The written assessment is a 30 minute written task based on the second simulated practice scenario you completed.

Booking your assessment

Once you have your manager's agreement, you can book your assessment.
